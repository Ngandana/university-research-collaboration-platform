# System Specification

## Project Title

University Research Collaboration Platform

## Domain

Education / Academic Research Management

## Domain Description

Universities conduct a significant amount of research involving collaboration between students, supervisors, and researchers. Research projects typically require coordination of multiple participants, sharing of documents, communication between team members, and monitoring of project progress.

Currently, many research collaborations rely on fragmented tools such as email, cloud storage platforms, and messaging applications. This fragmented environment makes it difficult to track project progress, manage research documentation, and maintain effective communication among research participants.

The University Research Collaboration Platform is designed to centralize research collaboration activities into a single integrated system that supports the management of research projects and collaboration among academic stakeholders.

## Problem Statement

Research collaboration within universities often lacks a centralized platform that allows students, supervisors, and researchers to effectively manage research projects and collaborate on academic work. The absence of such a platform leads to challenges such as poor coordination, document version conflicts, inefficient communication, and lack of transparency in project progress.

The proposed system aims to address these issues by providing a platform that enables structured collaboration, document management, communication, and project tracking for academic research activities.

## System Purpose

The purpose of the system is to provide a digital platform where research teams can collaboratively manage research projects, share documents, track tasks, and communicate effectively throughout the research lifecycle.

The platform will streamline the research workflow and improve collaboration between students and supervisors while maintaining organized documentation and project visibility.

## Target Users

The system will support the following user roles:

### Students

Students who are conducting research projects or theses and need to collaborate with supervisors and other research team members.

### Supervisors

Academic staff responsible for supervising student research and monitoring project progress.

### Researchers

Academic researchers who collaborate on research projects with students or other faculty members.

### System Administrators

Administrators responsible for managing system users, maintaining system integrity, and ensuring proper system operation.

## Core System Features

### User Account Management

The system will allow users to register, authenticate, and manage their profiles. Role-based access control will ensure that users can only access functions relevant to their roles.

### Research Project Management

Users will be able to create and manage research projects, define project goals, assign team members, and track project progress.

### Document Management

The platform will support uploading, sharing, and version control of research documents such as research proposals, datasets, reports, and publications.

### Task Management

Project members can assign tasks to team members, set deadlines, and monitor task completion.

### Communication and Notifications

The system will provide messaging and notification features to allow team members to communicate and stay informed about project updates.

### Collaboration Support

The platform will facilitate collaboration through shared workspaces where research teams can work together on documents and track changes.

## Functional Requirements

1. The system must allow users to create accounts and log in securely.
2. The system must allow administrators to manage user accounts.
3. The system must allow users to create and manage research projects.
4. The system must allow project owners to invite members to projects.
5. The system must allow users to upload and manage research documents.
6. The system must support document version tracking.
7. The system must allow tasks to be assigned to project members.
8. The system must allow users to communicate through messages or comments.
9. The system must send notifications for project updates.

## Non-Functional Requirements

### Security

The system must implement secure authentication and protect sensitive research data.

### Scalability

The system must support multiple concurrent users and growing numbers of research projects.

### Availability

The system should be accessible online and maintain reliable uptime.

### Usability

The system interface should be intuitive and easy to use for students and academic staff.

### Performance

The system should respond quickly to user requests and support efficient document uploads and downloads.

## Individual Scope

This project focuses on the specification and architectural design of the system rather than full implementation. The scope includes defining the system requirements, identifying system components, and designing the architecture using the C4 model.

The design will demonstrate how the system can be structured to support research collaboration in a scalable and maintainable way while remaining feasible for conceptual development within the scope of an individual academic project.
